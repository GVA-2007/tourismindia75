MOUNT-ABU

Situated amidst lush green, forested hills on the highest peak in the Aravali range, Mount Abu is the summer capital for the Indian state of Rajasthan. Its cool and soothing climate makes it an ideal retreat. Explore a number of Jain temples, including the Dilwara Temples, a complex carved of while marble, or visit the nearby Achalgarh fort, which houses many beautiful temples of its own.

Set amidst the lush greenery of the Aravalli mountains, places to visit in Mount Abu include waterfalls, lakes, forests, temples and scenic spots for sightseeing. Standing at 1722 m above the sea, Mount Abu is the only hill station in Rajasthan. Whether you visit the Dilwara Temple or Sunset Point, Mount Abu, never fails to amaze you with its beauty. Besides natural beauty, the place holds importance in history and mythology.

The name of the place comes from a mythical serpent named Arbhuda who had saved Lord Shivas bull, Nandi. This place is also believed to be the retreat of Sage Vashishtha. The various places to visit in Mount Abu connect visitors to this varied land of legends and history.
